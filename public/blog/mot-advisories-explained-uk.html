MOT Advisories Explained – What They Mean & When to Worry (UK)

MOT advisories are notes added during an MOT test to highlight parts of a vehicle that are worn, close to limits, or likely to need attention in the future. They are not failures, but ignoring them often leads to future MOT failures.

This guide explains MOT advisories clearly, how serious they are, and how to use them when buying or maintaining a car.

What is an MOT advisory?

An MOT advisory is recorded when a component:

  • Is worn but still legal
  • Is deteriorating and likely to fail soon
  • Should be monitored before the next MOT

The vehicle passes the MOT, but the advisory is a warning — not a guarantee of future safety.

Common MOT advisories you should not ignore

  • Brake pads or discs wearing thin
  • Suspension components showing wear
  • Tyres close to legal tread limit
  • Minor oil leaks
  • Corrosion beginning on chassis parts

Repeated advisories for the same issue year after year are a red flag.

MOT advisory vs MOT failure

Advisory: Vehicle is legal to drive, but attention is recommended.
Failure: Vehicle is not road legal until repaired.

Many failures start as advisories one or two years earlier.

Can you drive with MOT advisories?

Yes — but with caution.

An advisory does not make a car illegal to drive. However, if the condition worsens and becomes dangerous, you could still be stopped by police or invalidate insurance.

Should you buy a car with MOT advisories?

Yes — but only if:

  • The advisories are minor
  • They are not repeated every year
  • The price reflects future repair costs

A long advisory history for the same components often means poor maintenance.

How MOT advisories affect future MOT results

Advisories frequently become:

  • Failures in the next MOT
  • More expensive repairs later
  • Indicators of neglect

This is why checking patterns matters more than a single test result.

Frequently asked questions

Is an MOT advisory serious?

Some are minor, others are early warnings. Repeated advisories should always be taken seriously.

Do advisories affect insurance?

Not directly, but ignoring serious defects can invalidate claims after an accident.

Do MOT advisories expire?

No. They remain part of the vehicle’s MOT history permanently.

Final advice

MOT advisories are not something to fear — but they are not something to ignore either. Used correctly, they are one of the best tools for predicting maintenance costs and avoiding future MOT failures.

For a complete picture, always review the full MOT history alongside advisories.
