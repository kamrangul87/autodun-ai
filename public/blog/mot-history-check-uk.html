What is MOT history in the UK?

MOT UK history is the recorded timeline of a vehicle’s MOT tests. It typically includes pass/fail outcomes, advisory notes (items to monitor or repair soon), test dates, and the mileage recorded at each test. The most useful part of MOT history is not a single line — it is the pattern over time.

How to check MOT history online

  1. Enter the vehicle registration (number plate).
  2. Select Fetch MOT history.
  3. Review expiry date, mileage record, and previous failure count.
  4. Open the advisory/failure pattern and look for repeats year-to-year.

What MOT history shows (and what it doesn’t)

A typical MOT check history record can show:

  • MOT test results (pass/fail)
  • Advisories (items that may need attention)
  • Mileage recorded at the time of each test
  • Expiry date / timeline indicators

It does not usually confirm:

  • Whether repairs were completed (verify with receipts/service history)
  • Accident history, finance, or ownership history (separate checks)
  • Current condition between tests (issues can appear any time)

How to read advisories and failures properly

Advisories are not automatic failures, but they matter. A single advisory may be normal wear. Repeated advisories for the same area can indicate unresolved maintenance.

  • Look for repeats: tyres, brakes, suspension, emissions, lights.
  • Check escalation: a recurring advisory that becomes a failure later.
  • Compare mileage: consistent year-to-year progression matters.

Red flags to watch for when buying used

When checking mot history online before purchase, be cautious if you see:

  • Multiple failures for the same component area (e.g., brakes/emissions)
  • Large mileage jumps or long gaps with no records
  • Repeated advisories with no improvement year-on-year
  • A sudden “clean” result after years of repeats without evidence of work

MOT history vs MOT status

MOT status tells you whether the vehicle is currently legal to drive (and when the MOT expires). MOT history shows how the vehicle has performed over time — advisories, failures, and patterns.

Frequently asked questions

Is MOT history free in the UK?

Yes. MOT history is publicly accessible in the UK using the vehicle registration number. Tools may present the information differently, but the underlying record is based on UK testing data.

Can I check MOT history online without a V5C logbook?

Yes. A V5C logbook is not required to check MOT history online. A registration number is usually sufficient.

What does MOT history show?

MOT history typically includes pass/fail outcomes, advisory notes, test dates, recorded mileage at the time of test, and expiry information.

Does MOT history prove the car is in good condition today?

No. MOT history is a useful signal, but it does not guarantee current condition. Always combine MOT history with a physical inspection and service records.

How long does MOT history stay on record?

MOT history is generally available across the life of the vehicle, although the depth of older records can vary. Recent years are typically visible when checking MOT history online.

Can MOT history be changed or deleted?

MOT history is recorded at the time of testing. If there is a genuine error, it may be corrected through official channels, but results are not removed simply because repairs were completed later.

Why does mileage look wrong on MOT history?

Mileage differences can happen due to data entry errors, instrument replacement, or unusual usage patterns. Large or repeated inconsistencies should be investigated before buying a vehicle.

Does MOT history show accidents or insurance claims?

No. MOT history does not include accident or insurance data. It reflects the vehicle’s condition at the time of each MOT test only.

Do advisories mean the car will fail the next MOT?

Not always. Advisories highlight items to monitor. Repeated advisories for the same issue can increase the risk of a future failure if not addressed.
