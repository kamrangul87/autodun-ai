If you drive in London, knowing whether your car is ULEZ compliant is no longer optional. The Ultra Low Emission Zone now covers all London boroughs, and the rules apply every day. If your vehicle does not meet the emissions standard, you may need to pay a daily charge — and if you don’t pay, penalties can follow.

This guide explains what ULEZ is, how a ULEZ checker works, what “compliant” means for petrol and diesel cars, and how ULEZ compares to other UK clean-air schemes — in clear, practical terms.

What is ULEZ?

ULEZ stands for Ultra Low Emission Zone. It is a clean-air policy designed to reduce harmful vehicle emissions, especially nitrogen dioxide (NO2) from older petrol and diesel vehicles.

ULEZ is enforced by Transport for London (TfL) and operates 24/7, including weekends and public holidays.

Official reference: TfL — Ultra Low Emission Zone (ULEZ)

How the ULEZ checker works

A ULEZ checker uses your vehicle registration number (number plate) to look up DVLA-based vehicle information and confirm whether your vehicle meets the ULEZ emissions standard.

What you need:

  • Your vehicle registration number
  • That’s it — no VIN or paperwork required

Check your vehicle here (official): TfL vehicle checker

Which vehicles are ULEZ compliant?

ULEZ compliance is based on emissions standards (Euro standards). In simple terms:

  • Petrol: usually needs to meet Euro 4 (commonly petrol cars registered after ~2005, but always check)
  • Diesel: usually needs to meet Euro 6 (commonly diesel cars registered after ~September 2015, but always check)
  • Fully electric: compliant
  • Motorcycles: typically need Euro 3

Because there are exceptions, the safest approach is always to use the official ULEZ checker with your plate.

What happens if your car is not compliant?

If your vehicle is not compliant and you drive inside the ULEZ, a daily ULEZ charge may apply. If you don’t pay, TfL can issue a penalty charge notice (PCN).

Penalty information: Charges and PCN rules are published by TfL, and can change. Always confirm on official pages before relying on a third-party summary.

Official reference: TfL — ULEZ charges

ULEZ vs Clean Air Zones in the UK

ULEZ is London’s scheme, but other UK cities operate Clean Air Zones (CAZ) with different rules. A vehicle that is compliant in London may still face charges in a CAZ depending on the city and vehicle type.

Official reference: UK Government — Clean Air Zones

How to avoid ULEZ charges

If your vehicle is not compliant, your options typically include:

  • Use a compliant vehicle (newer petrol/diesel, or electric)
  • Switch to public transport for ULEZ journeys
  • Consider a retrofit (more common for some vans/specialist vehicles)
  • Pay the daily charge when driving in the zone

FAQs

Is my car automatically ULEZ compliant if it’s new?

Most newer cars are compliant, but “new” isn’t a guarantee. Use the official checker with your registration number to confirm.

Does ULEZ apply outside London?

ULEZ is London-only, but other UK cities operate Clean Air Zones with different rules and charging classes.

Do electric vehicles pay ULEZ charges?

No. Fully electric vehicles are ULEZ compliant.

Can visitors from outside London be fined?

Yes. ULEZ applies to all vehicles driving within the zone, regardless of where they’re registered.
