Why Use AI Mechanic? The Real-World Benefits

The honest answer is: time and money. A single diagnostic session at a garage typically costs between £50 and £100 before any work is done. For a problem that turns out to be minor — a loose petrol cap triggering an emissions warning, for instance — that's an expensive non-event. AI Mechanic gives you a credible first read for free.

There's also the urgency question. Not every warning light means pull over immediately. Some mean "book a service soon." Others mean stop the car now. Most drivers have no reliable way to tell the difference without calling a garage. The AI Mechanic addresses this directly, categorising issues by severity so you can make an informed decision about whether to drive, park up, or call for recovery. Some Example Questions — and What Good AI Answers Look Like

Understanding the range of questions AI Mechanic handles helps set realistic expectations. Good use cases include:

  • Interpreting dashboard warning lights (engine management, oil pressure, battery)
  • Diagnosing noises — knocking, squealing, rattling — by location and when they occur
  • Understanding MOT advisory items and whether they need immediate attention
  • Assessing whether a car is safe to drive a short distance to a garage
  • Getting a plain-English explanation of a fault code read by an OBD scanner

What the tool handles less well — and any honest review must say this — are faults that require physical inspection. A crack in a brake disc, a failing wheel bearing in its early stages, or a subtle oil leak won't always show clear symptoms. AI Mechanic is a first opinion, not a final diagnosis. The best approach is to use it to arrive at a garage informed, not to replace the garage entirely. That's not a limitation unique to this tool — it applies to every AI-powered diagnostic service currently available.
