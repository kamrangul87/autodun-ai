What the Official Guidance Says

The DVSA sets and enforces MOT standards in Great Britain. Full details of what testers check, and how defects are classified as dangerous, major, or minor, are published at gov.uk/getting-an-mot/what-happens-during-an-mot. Understanding these categories is genuinely useful — a "dangerous" defect means the vehicle should not be driven until repaired, whereas a "minor" defect is recorded but doesn't cause a fail.
