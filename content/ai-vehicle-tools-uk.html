Why Use AI Mechanic? The short answer is: time, money, and confidence. A standard garage diagnostic check typically costs between £50 and £100 before any repairs are quoted. Get Started with AI Mechanic Today: Common Mistakes to Avoid

Get Started with AI Mechanic Today is straightforward — visit the VehicleScore platform, select the AI Mechanic, and describe your issue. But a few practical mistakes are worth flagging before you do.

  • Don't describe the warning light alone. Tell the tool what the car was doing when the light appeared — idling, accelerating, after a cold start.
  • Include the vehicle's age and mileage. A gearbox rattle on a 12-year-old car with 110,000 miles has different probable causes than the same noise on a 3-year-old vehicle.
  • Don't dismiss low-severity ratings. If the AI flags something as low urgency, that's useful information — but "low urgency" doesn't mean "ignore indefinitely."
  • Use it before AND after a garage visit. Cross-checking a mechanic's diagnosis against the AI output is a smart habit, not a sign of distrust.

The tool is designed to empower you, not replace qualified mechanics. Treat it as your first opinion, not your only one.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does this work — does the AI Mechanic connect to my car directly?

No physical connection is needed. The AI Mechanic works entirely from the description you provide. You type or select your symptoms, and the system analyses them against fault pattern data. There's no OBD-II plug, no app, and no hardware required. This makes it accessible to any driver regardless of their car's age or technology level. The trade-off is that accuracy depends heavily on the detail you provide — the more specific your description, the more useful the output.

Is AI car advice reliable enough to act on?

Reliable as a starting point — yes. Reliable as a final diagnosis — no, and no reputable AI tool would claim otherwise. The honest answer is that AI vehicle tools give you an informed basis for a conversation with a qualified mechanic. They help you understand what might be wrong, how urgent it is, and what questions to ask. They don't replace a physical inspection. Used correctly, they save time and prevent both unnecessary panic and dangerous complacency.
